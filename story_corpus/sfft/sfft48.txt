<hr>  <h3 align="center">THE FAIRY'S SONG. <a name="fr_77"></a><a href="#fn_77"><font size="1">2</font></a>
</h3>  
<p>
</p>  
<p>"O where is tiny Hew?<br>   And where is little Len?<br> And where is bonnie Lu,<br>   And Menie of the Glen ?<br> And where's the place of rest--<br>   The ever changing hame ?<br> Is it the gowan's breast,<br>   Or 'neath the bells of faem?<br>     Ay lu lan dil y'u.</p>  
<p>"The fairest rose you find<br>   May have a taint within;<br> The flower of womankind<br>   May not be free from sin,--<br> The fox-glove cup go bring,<br>   The tail of shooting sterne, <a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="green">p. 151</font></a><br> And round our grassy ring<br>   We'll pledge the pith o' fern.<br>     Ay lu lan dil y'u.</p>  
<p>"And when the yellow moon<br>   Is gliding down the sky,<br> On wings of wishes boun',<br>   Our band to her can fly;<br> Her highest horn we'll ride,<br>   And quaff her honey dew;<br> Then in her shadowy side<br>   Our gambollings renew!<br>     Ay lu lan dil y'u.</p>  
<p> </p>

<h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
<p><a name="fn_77"></a><a href="sfft48.htm#fr_77">150:2</a> The song is taken from a poem founded upon the above story, and entitled the <i>Gloamyne Buchte</i>. The author was James Telfer, schoolmaster at Saughtree, in Liddesdale born 1800, died 1862.</p>
