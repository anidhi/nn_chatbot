<hr>  <h3 align="center">FROG AND CROW.</h3>  
<p>HERE is a bit of crow language,--a conversation with a frog. When it is repeated in Gaelic it can be made absurdly like the notes of the creatures.</p>  
<p><a name="page_95"><font size="1" color="green">p. 95</font></a></p>  
<p>"Ghille Criosda mhic Dhughail cuir a nois do mhàg."</p>  
<p>Christ's servant, son of Dugald, put up thy paw.</p>  
<p>"Tha eagal orm, tha eagal orm, tha eagal orm."</p>  
<p>I fear.</p>  
<p>"Gheibh thu còta gorm a's léine. Gheibh thu còta gorm a's léine."</p>  
<p>Thou shalt have a blue coat and a shirt.</p>  
<p>Then the frog put up his hand and the hoodie took him to a hillock and began to eat him, saying,</p>  
<p>"Biadh dona lom! 's bu dona riabh thu."</p>  
<p>Bad bare meat and bad wert thou ever.</p>  
<p>"Caite bheil do ghealladh math a nis?" said the frog.</p>  
<p>Where is thy good promise now?</p>  
<p>"Sann ag ol a bha sinn an latha sin. Sann ag ol a bha sinn an latha sin."</p>  
<p>It is drinking we were on that day.</p>  
<p>"Toll ort a ruid ghrannda gur beag feola tha air do chramhan."</p>  
<p>"Toll ort!" said the hoodie.</p>  
<p>A hole in thee, ugly thing! how little flesh is on thy bones.</p>
