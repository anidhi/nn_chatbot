Guleesh na Guss Dhu
(from this point onward the stories are all in English with no Gaelic text] THERE was once a boy in the County Mayo, and he never washed a foot from the day he was born. Guleesh was his name; but as nobody could ever prevail on him to wash his feet, they used to call him Guleesh na guss dhu, or Guleesh Black-foot. It's often the father said to him: "Get up, you  One night the whole family were gathered in by the fire, telling stories and making fun for themselves, and he amongst them. The father said to him: "Guleesh, you are one and twenty years old to-night, and I believe you never washed a foot from the day you were born till to-day." "You lie," said Guleesh, "didn't I go a' swimming on May day last? and I couldn't keep my feet out of the water." "Well, they were as dirty as ever they were when you came to the shore," said the father. "They were that, surely," said Guleesh. "That's the thing I'm saying," says the father, "that it wasn't in you to wash your feet ever." "And I never will wash them till the day of my death," said Guleesh.
